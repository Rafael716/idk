# 9.4 Speaking and writing

## Language for Writing – A Formal Letter

### Greetings and endings
- If we don’t know the name of the person, we use:  
  **Dear Sir/Madam → Yours faithfully**

- If we know the name of the person, we use:  
  **Dear Mrs Al-Sayed → Yours sincerely**

### Explaining the reason for writing
- I am writing to … (apply for, request)
- I would like to …
- I wish to …

### Explaining what you have sent
- Please find enclosed …
- I enclose …
- As requested, I enclose …

### Polite expressions to close a letter
- I am happy to provide any additional information that you need.
- I look forward to hearing from you.
- I hope to hear from you soon.


## Language for Speaking – Asking for help and giving advice

### Giving advice
- You must/mustn’t (do) …
- You should/shouldn’t (do) …
- I think/I don’t think you should (do) …
- You could try (doing) …
- It’s a good idea to (do) …

*Note: We often use “I think / I don’t think” before “should” to sound more polite.*

### Asking for help
- Have you got anything for …?
- Could you give me something for …?

### Offering help
- What can I do for you?
- How can I help you?
