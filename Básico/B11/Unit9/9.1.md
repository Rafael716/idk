## GRAMMAR FOCUS: if + present simple + will/won’t/might

- We use **if + present** tense to talk about a **consequence** action or situation and we use **will, won’t or might** to talk about the **possible** result of the action or situation.  
  *Example:* If your handshake is too strong, you will seem rude.  
  ... they might catch an illness if they shake someone’s hand.

- The **if** clause can come first or second. If it comes **first**, we put a comma after it.

- If we are not certain about the result, we can use **might** instead of will/won’t.

### ¿Para qué sirve esta estructura?

Usamos **if + present simple** para hablar de una **condición real o posible**.  
Luego usamos **will**, **won’t** o **might** para expresar el **resultado futuro** de esa condición.

- **Will** → resultado seguro o muy probable  
- **Won’t** → resultado negativo seguro o muy probable  
- **Might** → resultado posible pero no seguro

---

### 1. If + Present = condición real

La parte que empieza con **if** describe una acción o situación que **puede ocurrir en el presente o futuro**.

**Ejemplos:**  
- *If your handshake is too strong,* → condición  
- *If they shake someone’s hand,* → condición

---

### 2. Will / Won’t / Might = resultado posible

La segunda parte muestra lo que **ocurrirá** dependiendo de la condición.

**Ejemplos:**  
- *… you **will** seem rude.* (resultado casi seguro)  
- *… they **might** catch an illness.* (resultado posible)

---

### 3. ¿Dónde va la parte “if”?

La cláusula **if** puede ir:

#### ✔ Al inicio  
Se usa una **coma (,)** después de la condición.

> **If** your handshake is too strong, you will seem rude.

#### ✔ Al final  
No se usa coma.

> You will seem rude **if** your handshake is too strong.

Ambas formas son correctas.

---

### 4. ¿Cuándo usar *might*?

Usamos **might** cuando el resultado **no es seguro**, solo **posible**.

- Resultado seguro:  
  *If you eat too much, you **will** feel sick.*

- Resultado posible:  
  *If you eat too much, you **might** feel sick.*

---

### ⭐ Resumen simple

| Parte de la oración | Tiempo verbal | Función |
|---------------------|---------------|---------|
| **If + present simple** | (If you eat…) | Condición |
| **Will / Won’t / Might + verbo base** | (…you will feel) | Resultado futuro |

## EJEMPLOS

#### Will
  - Sentences
    1. If you study hard, you will pass the exam.
    2. If it rains, we will stay inside.
    3. If she calls me, I will answer right away.
    4. You will feel better if you rest.
    5. We will arrive on time if we leave now
    6. She will help you if you ask her.
  - Question
    1. If you study hard, will you pass the exam?
    2. If it rains, will we stay inside?
    3. If she calls, will you answer right away?
    4. Will you feel better if you rest?
    5. Will we arrive on time if we leave now?
    6. Will she help you if you ask her?


#### Won't
  - Sentences
    1. If you don’t eat, you won’t have energy.
    2. If he doesn’t study, he won’t pass the test.
    3. If they don’t hurry, they won’t catch the bus.
    4. You won’t improve if you never practice.
    5. He won’t understand the lesson if he doesn’t pay attention.
    6. They won’t go out if it rains.
  - Question
    1. If you don’t eat, won’t you lose energy?
    2. If he doesn’t study, won’t he fail the test?
    3. If they don’t hurry, won’t they miss the bus?
    4. Won’t you improve if you practice more?
    5. Won’t he understand better if he pays attention?
    6. Won’t they stay home if it rains?


#### Might
  - Sentences
    1. If it gets cold, we might stay home.
    2. If you call her, she might come to the party.
    3. If he finishes early, he might join us.
    4. We might cancel the trip if the weather is bad.
    5. She might buy the dress if it’s on sale.
    6. I might go out later if I finish my work.
  - Question
    1. If it gets cold, might we stay home?
    2. If you call her, might she come to the party?
    3. If he finishes early, might he join us?
    4. Might we cancel the trip if the weather is bad?
    5. Might she buy the dress if it’s on sale?
    6. Might you go out later if you finish your work?
