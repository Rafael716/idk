## GRAMMAR FOCUS: if + present simple + will/won’t/might

- We use **if + present** tense to talk about a **consequence** action or situation and we use **will, won’t or might** to talk about the **possible** result of the action or situation.  
  *Example:* If your handshake is too strong, you will seem rude.  
  ... they might catch an illness if they shake someone’s hand.

- The **if** clause can come first or second. If it comes **first**, we put a comma after it.

- If we are not certain about the result, we can use **might** instead of will/won’t.
